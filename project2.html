Home

Every year, over 40,000 tons of wool are produced in Kazakhstan. Yet, 73.5% remains unprocessed, leading to environmental challenges. Our project aims to transform this waste into eco-friendly wool pellets that enhance soil moisture and deliver essential nutrients.

About the Project

This project, led by young innovators from IQanat High School of Burabay, focuses on developing a sustainable method to convert raw wool waste into organic fertilizer. Through research and practical trials, we have validated the benefits of wool pellets in agriculture.

Benefits & Infographics

Natural Fertilizer

100% natural composition, safe and effective for soil health.

Enhanced Water Retention

Can retain up to 20 times its weight in water, reducing irrigation needs.

Slow Nutrient Release

Gradually releases essential nutrients like nitrogen and potassium for sustained plant growth.

Project Results

Trials have shown that wool pellets improve soil moisture retention and enhance plant development. International experiences, such as those from Ireland, confirm the efficiency and environmental benefits of using wool-based fertilizers.

Future Plans

We plan to continue our research, collaborate with local farmers, and attract governmental and private investment to scale up production. Our future trials will further refine the nutrient composition and delivery system of wool pellets.
